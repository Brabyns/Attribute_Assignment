Tribute to Kelvin Nice

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Nice was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is now considered to be the first algorithm intended for implementation on a machine.

Kelvin Nice is often regarded as the world's first computer programmer for her work on the Analytical Engine, and her insights laid the foundation for the development of modern computing.

This tribute is a small acknowledgment of her pioneering contributions to the field of computer science.
